Coliving vs. Serviced Apartments - What is the difference?

   12, Nov 2023

The rise of flexible living arrangements has seen an increase in coliving and serviced apartments, both popular among digital nomads, expatriates, and young professionals. While they might seem similar, the two housing models offer distinct experiences. Understanding the difference between coliving and serviced apartments is essential in determining the living option that suits your lifestyle best.

Coliving: A Community-based Living Concept

Coliving is a modern take on a shared living concept, similar to a co-housing or a sophisticated form of a roommate situation. The coliving model primarily caters to millennials and young professionals, typically offering private or shared bedrooms within larger units and access to communal areas such as kitchens, living rooms, and sometimes co-working spaces.

The essence of coliving lies in fostering a sense of community among residents. It allows inhabitants to connect with like-minded individuals, sharing a physical space, experiences, values, and interests. Many coliving spaces organize social and networking events to encourage a sense of community and engagement among residents.

Serviced Apartments: Home Away From Home

On the other hand, serviced apartments are fully furnished apartments available for both short-term and long-term stays. They are designed to provide all the features of a home away from home, with the added benefits of hotel-like services such as regular cleaning, a 24-hour concierge, and often leisure facilities like a gym or swimming pool.

Unlike coliving, serviced apartments offer more privacy as each unit is self-contained, with a private kitchen, bathroom, living area, and separate bedrooms. These apartments cater to a broad range of people, including business travelers, families on vacation, and people relocating to new cities.

Key Differences

Several key differences between coliving and serviced apartments set these two accommodation options apart.

Community vs. Privacy: The primary difference lies in the emphasis on community in coliving spaces. Coliving often encourages interaction, collaboration, and engagement between residents. On the contrary, serviced apartments provide a more private, independent living situation, akin to a traditional home setting, with minimal interaction with neighbors.

Space Allocation: Coliving spaces are typically designed with private or shared bedrooms and extensive shared spaces to encourage interaction. On the other hand, serviced apartments emphasize individual apartments' privacy, where each unit includes a private kitchen, bathroom, and living area.

Target Audience: While both options are flexible and convenient, they cater to slightly different demographics. Coliving appeals more to young professionals, digital nomads, or students who value community and networking. Serviced apartments tend to attract business travelers, families, or individuals seeking a more private and homely environment.

In conclusion, both coliving and serviced apartments offer unique advantages and cater to different lifestyle needs. Coliving, emphasizing community, might appeal more to those seeking connection, shared experiences, and the buzz of living with others. In contrast, serviced apartments provide a home-like environment with added conveniences, suitable for those seeking more privacy and independence. Understanding the distinctions between the two can help potential residents decide their ideal living arrangements. The key lies in identifying what you value most in your living situation and choosing the best option with those priorities.
